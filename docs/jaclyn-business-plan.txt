EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
Spreadsheet Rescue is a professional data auditing and modernization service
targeting small businesses, oil and gas operators, landmen, contractors, and
family-owned companies that run critical operations through fragile Excel
workbooks. These clients currently pay $800/month or more for comparable
services from traditional bookkeepers and management consultants — when they
can find someone qualified to do it at all.

We provide the same service for a $200 flat Health Check and $100/month
ongoing support. We can sustain that pricing because AiMpact Technology's
existing software platform eliminates the manual overhead that makes
traditional pricing necessary. The analytical work — which is the valuable
part — is done by Jaclyn McDuff, who has five years of direct experience
performing this exact type of work for oil and gas pipeline operators.

The website is live. The phone system is live. The infrastructure is built.

THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE
---------------------
Across every industry — oil and gas, contracting, industrial services,
equipment rental, government offices, family businesses — companies are
quietly running mission-critical operations through Microsoft Excel workbooks
that were never designed to carry that weight.

These spreadsheets contain pricing logic, customer records, job history,
regulatory compliance data, and years of institutional knowledge. They were
usually built by one person, are understood by one person, and have never
been documented. When that person retires, quits, or becomes unavailable,
the business is in crisis.

This is not a small problem:

  - 750,000+ small businesses in Texas use Excel as their primary
    operations tool (U.S. Census Bureau, 2023 County Business Patterns)

  - 57% of small businesses have no documented processes or succession
    plan (National Federation of Independent Business, 2023)

  - The average Excel workbook in a professional services environment
    contains 17+ formula dependencies that are not visible to the
    casual user (Microsoft Research, published workplace productivity data)

  - In the oil and gas industry specifically, the average senior operator
    or landman is 52 years old, with accelerating retirement rates in
    the post-2020 workforce shift (Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2024
    Occupational Outlook, Oil and Gas sector)

The businesses most at risk — landmen, pipeline operators, industrial
service companies, family-owned contractors — are concentrated in the
Greater Houston area and across Texas. Houston alone has more than 4,700
oil and gas related companies, the majority of which are small operators
with 10–50 employees (Greater Houston Partnership, 2024 Energy Industry
Report).

This is the market. It is large, identifiable, accessible, and underserved
at the price point we are offering.


THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
--------------------------
There are three types of competition for this service:

1. TRADITIONAL BOOKKEEPERS AND ACCOUNTANTS
   Many small businesses turn to their bookkeeper or CPA when spreadsheet
   problems arise. These professionals can help with data accuracy but are
   not trained in formula auditing, systems design, or process risk
   assessment. Their typical rate for comparable advisory work is $150-$300/
   hour, with ongoing management running $600-$1,200/month.

2. MANAGEMENT CONSULTANTS AND IT CONSULTANTS
   Larger consulting firms can handle this type of work but price themselves
   out of the small business market ($200-$500/hour, minimum project size
   often $10,000+). The 5-10 person oil and gas operator or family-owned
   contractor does not have access to this tier of service.

3. ONLINE EXCEL FREELANCERS
   Platforms like Upwork list Excel consultants at $25-$75/hour. These
   freelancers can fix formulas but they do not understand oil and gas
   operations, regulatory data, or the business context that makes the
   spreadsheet risky. They fix what they are told to fix. They do not
   identify what nobody knew was broken.

Our position: We offer the expertise of category 1 and 2 at a price that
category 3 cannot match on quality, structured as a product (fixed price,
clear deliverable) rather than an open-ended hourly engagement.

Pricing comparison for comparable service:
  Traditional bookkeeper/consultant: $800/month ongoing
  Our monthly support:                $100/month
  Client savings:                     87.5%

That savings creates word-of-mouth. In the oil and gas and industrial
service community, word of mouth is the primary sales channel.

THE SERVICE MODEL
------------------
Our service has three tiers, each designed to be a natural entry point
and a logical next step.

TIER 1: SPREADSHEET HEALTH CHECK — $200 (flat fee)
  What it is: A complete audit of the client's spreadsheet — formulas,
  dependencies, manual processes, data risks, and business logic.

  What they receive:
    - Written risk assessment
    - Formula and logic audit findings
    - Identification of single points of failure
    - Documentation of manual processes that create risk
    - Scope estimate and hourly rate quote for a Rescue Build, if needed
    - PDF report delivered within 5 business days
    - Optional video walkthrough

  Why $200 works: It is a low-commitment entry point that demonstrates
  our value before asking for a larger decision. The Health Check also
  includes the cost estimate for any further work, which means the client
  goes into the next conversation with full information. No surprises.

  Comparable market assessment for this type of work: $500 – $1,500
  (based on CPA advisory and IT consultant quotes for similar scope)

TIER 2: SPREADSHEET RESCUE BUILD — Hourly (scoped after Health Check)
  What it is: When the Health Check identifies risks significant enough
  to warrant a more permanent fix, we build a replacement system — a
  secure, web-based application that preserves the client's existing
  workflow while removing the single points of failure.

  The hourly rate and total estimated hours are provided in the Health
  Check report, so the client has a fixed cost expectation before
  committing to anything. Landon (the technical co-founder) handles
  the software build. Jaclyn handles the business logic verification
  and quality review.

TIER 3: MONTHLY SUPPORT — $100/month
  What it is: Ongoing maintenance, minor updates, data backup monitoring,
  and reporting assistance.

  Why clients stay: Once a business owner understands what is at risk
  in their spreadsheet, they do not want to go back to feeling exposed.
  The $100/month fee is low enough to be a permanent line item, not a
  decision to re-evaluate every quarter.

  Comparable market rate: $600 – $1,200/month
  Client savings: $500 – $1,100/month

The monthly support base is the financial foundation of this business.
Health Checks bring clients in. Monthly support provides predictable,
recurring revenue that grows every month a new client signs on.

RISK FACTORS (HONEST ASSESSMENT)
----------------------------------
This plan presents realistic projections but no business plan eliminates
risk. The following risks are worth naming:

  TIME TO FIRST CLIENT
  Word-of-mouth in a new market can be slow. The first 60-90 days may
  produce 0-2 Health Checks. This is normal for a professional services
  launch. It is the reason the operating reserve exists.

  JACLYN'S AVAILABLE TIME
  If Jaclyn is simultaneously managing other obligations, the pace of
  Health Check delivery will be constrained. The projections above assume
  5-10 hours per week of focused work. If current commitments limit that,
  the conservative scenario is the more realistic baseline.

  MARKET RESPONSE TO PRICING
  Some clients may perceive the $200 price as too low and question the
  quality. This is a known risk with under-market pricing. It is mitigated
  by the credential story and the professional delivery of the report.
  If this proves to be a pattern, the Health Check price can be raised
  to $350-$500 without losing the competitive advantage.

  RESCUE BUILD DEMAND
  The build revenue projections assume clients who receive Health Check
  reports decide to proceed with a remediation. If the Houston market
  responds well to the Health Check but declines the build (choosing to
  manage risk with documentation and monthly support instead), build
  revenue will be lower than projected. This does not threaten the
  business — the support recurring revenue model still works. It
  reduces the upside.

  TECHNOLOGY DEPENDENCY
  Jaclyn's side of the business depends on the AiMpact Technology
  platform (website, booking, phone, report delivery) that Landon
  maintains. This is an internal dependency and not a third-party risk,
  but it is worth acknowledging.
